I'm interested in trust infrastructures, systems thinking, innovation in India, working with great people, and using technology to give myself more time to be bored — that's when the best ideas happen. I have a bias towards building, and I love writing, teaching, reading and learning.

Hoovu Fresh

Hoovu is a tech-enabled supply chain for puja flowers — the garlands and loose blooms used in temples, weddings, and homes across India. We operate in nine cities and run at roughly ₹20 crore in revenue. My sister Yeshoda is the CEO. We were on Shark Tank India in Season 2.

I run engineering and strategy. Most of my work right now is building the internal AI layer that turns six years of operational knowledge into something the team can actually use.

Building

Small, personal tools. Most of these live as private experiments I share with friends.

  • Chapel A cross-medium personal library that tracks the emotional impact of art — everything that shapes you, in one place.
  • Cahoots A two-person goal accountability app. For you and your person.
  • The Brief News without the overwhelm. A reader for people who want to be informed, not flooded.
  • The Brief — Personal OS A front page for your day.
  • The Brief — Inbox Warm emails. Email that feels like a letter, not a queue.
  • Vendetta Your reasons to leave the house. A ‘do disturb’ signal so friends can meet serendipitously without the planning.
  • ComPrice Commodity pricing built on Indian APMC government data. Don't compromise with your data.
  • Pitchin Club A covid project to help raise funds for your favourite charity by volunteering your time.

Background

I studied Science, Technology and Society at Stanford. My honors thesis was on Aadhaar (presentation) — trust infrastructure as a lens has stayed with me. Before Hoovu I wrote for newspapers, taught introductory CS, and spent time in art museums and craft villages trying to understand how things get made.

I split my time between Bangalore and San Francisco. I read mostly fiction and philosophy.
